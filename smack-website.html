FAQ

Good questions.

Answers to things studios actually ask.

What kinds of studios is smack. built for?
smack. is built for commercial and branded content production studios — the kind running 3–20 concurrent projects, managing a roster of freelance crew, sending invoices to agencies and brands, and needing clients to approve deliverables without emailing a Vimeo link and waiting three days. If you're scheduling shoots in Google Calendar and tracking budgets in a spreadsheet, smack. is for you.
What's the difference between a team member and a contractor?
Team members are your full-time or core studio staff who have full smack. access — they can create projects, manage crew, and send invoices. Contractors are the freelancers you hire project-to-project. They show up in your crew roster, can be booked onto projects, and receive call sheets — but they don't need a paid seat. All plans include unlimited contractor seats.
How does the client portal work?
Each project gets its own client-facing portal — a clean, minimal view showing project status, phases, and deliverables. Your client can approve or comment on deliverables without needing a smack. account. Portals are shareable via a private link. On the Pro plan, portals carry your studio's branding instead of smack.'s.
Do contractors need to create an account?
No. Contractors receive booking confirmations and call sheets via email — no login required. If you want to give a contractor ongoing access to their schedule or documents, you can invite them to a limited contractor view. Either way, they never need to be a paid seat.
Is there a free trial?
During our invite-only launch, we're onboarding studios selectively rather than running a traditional free trial. If you apply and we think it's a fit, we'll bring you in and work with you directly for your first 30 days. No credit card required to apply.
When is smack. launching?
We're in an invite-only phase right now, onboarding studios one at a time to make sure the product is tight before we open more broadly. Apply via the form on this page — we'll be in touch within a few business days if it looks like a good fit. Public launch is later this year.
