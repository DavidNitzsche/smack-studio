Logo v6 — Wide Open
Twelve new directions.
Nothing is locked. These are all wrong until one is right.
Group 1 — Shape explorations
V6-1
The Floating Cap
Flying-saucer dome, long trailing tentacles. Drama in contrast.
V6-2
The Tall Bell
Thimble proportion. The bell is the statement — tentacles are secondary.
V6-3
Asymmetric
Tilted 4°. Mid-swim. Gives it intention, not just shape.
V6-4
Negative Space Ring
Jellyfish contained inside a circle frame. Stamp-like authority.
Group 2 — Tentacle explorations
V6-5
Tapered Strokes
Tentacles taper to a point. Precise, graphic, vector-clean.
V6-6
Ribbon Tentacles
Flat filled ribbons, gently waving. Fluid and organic.
V6-7
Dot Tentacles
Tentacles as cascading dot columns. Abstract, graphic, poster-ready.
V6-8
Two-Length Alternating
Long-short-long-short-long. Rhythm. The eye bounces through it.
Group 3 — Conceptual / logo-thinking
V6-9
The Letterform
The S IS the jellyfish. Body as glyph, tentacles below the bowl.
V6-10
Overlapping Circles
Three intersecting discs form the dome. Venn logic, jellyfish silhouette.
